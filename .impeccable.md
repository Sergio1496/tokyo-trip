## Design Context

### Users
Two travelers (the creator and their companion) consulting the app on their phones during a 14-day trip to Tokyo. High-stress context: navigating a foreign city, often standing on a train platform or walking. Speed and clarity are everything — they need the right info in under 3 seconds.

### Brand Personality
**Practical, direct, confident.** No fluff, no decoration for decoration's sake. The interface is a tool, not a showcase. It should feel like a well-organized personal notebook, not a tourism brochure.

3-word personality: **Clear. Fast. Useful.**

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Tone**: Utilitarian minimal — inspired by Notion/Linear's restraint and typography-driven hierarchy
- **Theme**: Light and dark mode, zinc-based neutrals with category-specific color coding (orange=food, red=temple, sky=attraction)
- **Anti-references**: Tourism websites with hero images, overly playful/emoji-heavy apps, dense Japanese transit apps
- **What makes it memorable**: Information density done right — every pixel earns its place

### Design Principles
1. **Info in 3 seconds** — The most important data (where to go, how to get there, what it costs) must be visible without scrolling or tapping
2. **Hierarchy over decoration** — Use typography weight, size, and color to guide the eye. Never add a visual element that doesn't carry information
3. **Mobile-first, mobile-only** — Design for one-handed phone use while standing. Touch targets, readable text, no hover-dependent interactions
4. **Color means something** — Every color communicates category, status, or urgency. No decorative color
5. **Progressive disclosure** — Show essentials first, details on demand. Don't overwhelm with everything at once
